Blueprint: Chaos Engineering

Chaos Engineering is the discipline of experimenting on a system by intentionally injecting controlled failures to build confidence in its ability to withstand turbulent, real-world conditions.

The Scientific Method of Chaos

Effective chaos engineering follows a structured, scientific process to learn from failures safely.

  1. Define a "Steady State": Establish a measurable baseline of normal system behavior.
  2. Formulate a Hypothesis: State a clear, falsifiable assumption about the system's resilience.
  3. Inject Real-World Failures: Introduce controlled faults like pod deletion, network latency, or CPU exhaustion.
  4. Attempt to Disprove the Hypothesis: Observe if the system deviates from its steady state. If it doesn't, confidence increases. If it does, a weakness is found.
  5. Minimize the "Blast Radius": Start experiments small and in pre-production, ensuring containment.

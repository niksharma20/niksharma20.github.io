Blueprint: Rate Limiting & Throttling

To protect services from being overwhelmed by excessive traffic—whether malicious or simply unexpected—it is essential to implement controls at the edge of the cluster.

Rate Limiting vs. Throttling

These related techniques are implemented at the API Gateway or Ingress Controller to protect backend services from traffic floods.

Rate Limiting

Enforces a hard cap on requests. If the limit is exceeded, subsequent requests are rejected (e.g., HTTP 429). Its goal is to enforce usage policies and prevent abuse.

Throttling

A dynamic technique to regulate request flow. It slows down or queues requests to prevent overwhelming the system, smoothing out bursts to maintain availability.

Rate Limiting as a Resiliency Pattern

Beyond security, rate limiting is a fundamental resiliency pattern. It functions as a programmatic bulkhead at the network edge, shielding backend services from traffic spikes that could cause cascading failures.

Protecting Backend Services

By enforcing a hard cap on requests per second, a rate limiter ensures that application pods are never subjected to a higher load than they are provisioned to handle. This prevents resource exhaustion, pod crashes, and OOMKills, enhancing the stability of the entire system.
